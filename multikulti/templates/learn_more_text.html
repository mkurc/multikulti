About cabs-dock

Introduction

So far, the major unsolved problem in protein docking is the treatment of proteins with substantial backbone conformational change. Based on the coarse-grained (cg) cabs model, we developed and present a cabs-dock web server for docking of peptides to proteins. Due to high efficiency of the cg approach, during molecular docking, cabs-dock treats peptide backbone as fully flexible, while the flexibility of receptor proteins is limited to near-native backbone fluctuations.

The cabs-dock method was developed, optimized and validated during the following simulation experiments:

  1. simulations of folding and binding of an intrinsically disordered peptide [ ref 1];
  2. docking of antigen-mimicking peptides to antibody [ ref 2];
  3. docking of peptide co-activators to the nuclear receptors [ ref 3-4].
In the all above mentioned validation tests peptides conformation were allowed to be fully flexible and no information about the binding site nor the peptide conformation was used. The docking results showed that the method is able to predict the near native complex arrangements. The validity of near-native dynamics of the cabs model was also investigated in the recent studies [ ref 5-6].

Method description

The only data required as an input is peptide sequence and protein structure in pdb format (or a protein pdb code). The protein structure is used as a starting point for the cabs simulation of near-native dynamics. The resulted docking trajectory is analyzed and processed to provide useful description of modeling results in the following outputs:

  • a set of representative protein models (in an all-atom resolution)
  • docking trajectory in cg format
An important attribute of cg protein models generated by the cabs is that their spatial resolution allows for reconstruction to all-atom, physically realistic models.
